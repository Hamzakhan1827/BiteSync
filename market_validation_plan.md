# CraveSync Market Validation & Research Strategy

This document outlines the strategic steps to validate the **CraveSync** concept, brand, and features before a full market push. The goal is to move from "I think this is a problem" to "I have data that proves this is a problem."

---

## 1. Core Hypotheses to Validate

### Hypothesis A: The "Rating Paradox"
*   **The Idea**: People trust 4-star restaurants but are often disappointed by specific dishes.
*   **Validation Question**: "How many times have you visited a top-rated restaurant only to have a mediocre meal because you ordered the 'wrong' thing?"
*   **Goal**: Prove that **Dish-First** discovery is more valuable than **Restaurant-First** discovery.

### Hypothesis B: The "Feedback Black Hole"
*   **The Idea**: Restaurant owners want honest feedback but only get "Star Ratings" which don't help them improve.
*   **Validation Question**: (To Restaurant Owners) "When you see a 3-star review, do you actually know which dish or which specific ingredient failed the customer?"
*   **Goal**: Prove that the **Message to Chef** feature is a B2B selling point.

### Hypothesis C: The "Digital Junk Drawer"
*   **The Idea**: People take photos of food and want to remember what they ate, but their camera roll is a mess.
*   **Validation Question**: "How do you currently keep track of the dishes you want to re-order vs. the ones you never want to see again?"
*   **Goal**: Prove that the **Private Food Diary** is a high-utility daily habit.

---

## 2. Research Channels & Communities

### Online Communities (Reddit)
*   **r/foodies**: Best for testing the "Diary" and "Dish Discovery" angle. Ask about their "Worst best meal" experiences.
*   **r/Restaurateurs / r/Chefs**: Best for testing the "Message to Chef" feature. Ask them about their frustration with vague Google/Yelp reviews.
*   **Local Subreddits (e.g., r/Lahore, r/London, etc.)**: Ask about the reliability of local ratings.

### Social Media (Facebook Groups)
*   **Local Foodie Groups**: High-intent users. Run polls: "Do you look at the restaurant rating or do you look for photos of specific dishes before ordering?"

### Direct Outreach (Interviews)
*   **The "3-Table Test"**: Visit 3 local partner restaurants. Ask 5 customers if they would prefer their feedback to go directly to the chef rather than a public star rating.

---

## 3. Actionable Research Questions (Copy-Paste)

### For Consumers (Social Media/Reddit):
> "Does anyone else find Google Maps ratings completely useless lately? I went to a 4.8-star place last night and the Karahi was actually terrible, but the place looks 'cool' so it has high stars. How do you guys find out what is actually GOOD to order before you go?"

### For Restaurant Owners (LinkedIn/Reddit):
> "Question for chefs/owners: When you get a 1-star or 3-star review on Yelp/Google, do you ever find out exactly which dish was the problem? Does that data actually reach the kitchen, or is it just a PR headache?"

---

## 4. Success Metrics for Launch
*   **Validation Success**: 70%+ of people surveyed agree that they look for specific dish photos/reviews over general restaurant ratings.
*   **B2B Interest**: 3 out of 5 local restaurant owners express interest in a "Direct to Chef" dashboard.
*   **Retention Hook**: Users who log at least 3 "Diary" entries in a week are 50% more likely to return.
